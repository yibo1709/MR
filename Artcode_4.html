Wollaton Park Lake

The 25-acre lake was originally created in the late 18th century as part of the landscaping of the Wollaton Park estate. The lake was formed by damming a small stream that ran through the park, creating a picturesque body of water that became a focal point of the park’s landscape.

If you want get more infomation, please continue to the official website: https://friendsofwollatonpark.org.uk/wollaton-park/wollaton-park-lake/

Animals at Wollaton Park Lake:

Wollaton Park Lake is home to a variety of wildlife, including waterfowl, swans, and various species of ducks. The surrounding parkland provides shelter to squirrel, deer, and other wildlife. It’s a perfect spot for birdwatching and nature enthusiasts.
